About this book

Most language learners study one language at a time, in isolation. How to Be Multilingual FAST takes a radically different approach: by studying multiple languages comparatively, you leverage the structural similarities between them to learn all of them faster. This book provides a unified framework for language acquisition — a systematic method that shows how languages are built, what they share, and how to exploit those patterns to become multilingual efficiently. Whether you are starting your second language or your fifth, this book gives you the map.

What is inside

🗺️

A comparative framework

A structural overview of how languages are built — what they share and how they differ across language families.

⚡

Accelerated acquisition
